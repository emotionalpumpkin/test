Colossal Squid

Mariana Snailfish
Pseudoliparis swirei
The deepest-dwelling vertebrate on record, its transparent gelatinous body allows ambient pressure to pass through rather than crush it.

200 – 4,000m
Atolla Jellyfish
Atolla wyvillei
When attacked, this crimson medusa triggers a spectacular bioluminescent alarm — a spinning blue wheel of light designed to attract larger predators to scare away its attacker.

2,000 – 5,000m
Rimicaris Shrimp
Rimicaris exoculata
Blind but not sightless — these shrimp possess photoreceptors on their backs to detect the faint thermal glow of hydrothermal vents, guiding them home in total darkness.

300 – 2,000m
Dumbo Octopus
Grimpoteuthis boylei
Propelled by two ear-like fins, this ghostly octopus is among the rarest ever filmed. It swallows prey whole — the only member of its genus to do so.

1,500 – 3,000m
Pacific Barreleye
Macropinna microstoma
Its transparent dome-shaped head encloses glowing tubular eyes that can rotate within the skull, always scanning upward through its own forehead for prey silhouettes.

Field Notes from
the Trench

Feb 2026 Mariana Trench
11,034m
Three Unknown Species Documented Near Challenger Deep
ROV Hadal-7 returned footage of an unclassified polychaete worm exhibiting patterned bioluminescent signaling behavior at approximately 10,800m — behavior not previously observed at these pressures. Two additional specimens await morphological analysis.
New Discovery
Nov 2025 Tonga Trench
9,100m
Microplastic Concentration Survey — Year 3
Sediment cores extracted from the Tonga Trench confirm continued accumulation of polyethylene and polypropylene fibers at hadal depths. Concentration has increased 14% year-over-year, with fragments detected in the gut tissue of amphipod specimens.
Environmental Survey
Aug 2025 Kermadec Trench
8,600m
Chemosynthetic Community Mapped at Active Seep Site
A 200-meter-wide cold seep community discovered at 8,600m, supporting dense populations of chemosynthetic bacteria, siboglinid tube worms, and an unprecedented density of methane-consuming archaea. The site is now under continuous monitoring.
